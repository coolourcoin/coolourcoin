1. Thesis & Principles

Thesis. Most crypto is volatile and opaque. CoolourCoin makes value visible and grounded in perception: colour. A colour exists once, and once priced, its floor is never lowered again. The protocol encodes this protection.

Principles

  • Clarity: no passive yield, no staking promises, no inflation tricks.
  • Finality: floor is monotonic ↑ only; we never add backdoors.
  • Human-first: mining requires a small social team (R, G, B), not hardware farms.
  • Open: contracts and rules are open-source; the colour release seed is private but audit-verifiable.
Ticker (tentative): COLR. Asset type: non-fungible “Spectral Assets” (1 shade = 1 token ID).

2. Genesis Spectrum

We start with a finite, curated set of 4,096 shades (the Genesis). More can appear later via explicit expansion rules (by octaves, i.e., ×8 splits) only if community value justifies it.

Tiers

  • Tier 1: White apex (unique).
  • Tier 2: Primary anchors (R, G, B) + a few near-white rarities.
  • Tier 3–4: Structured derivatives and rare gradations.

Each shade has: id, name, HEX/RGB, tier, rarity.

3. Mining Model (RGB Teams & Licences)

Team mining. A colour is claimed only by a present trio: one Red, one Green, one Blue miner (each with a valid licence). The team targets an exact shade (e.g., R200 G128 B099). No multi-targeting; one team → one shade at a time.

Licences

Each miner holds a channel licence (R or G or B). A licence has a capacity up to 255 units for its channel. When a shade is mined, the team’s capacities are spent exactly on those channel amounts; the remainder stays on the licence for the next attempts.

Disconnect rule. If any team member disconnects, the team has a 15-minute grace period to reconnect; otherwise the shade window is released to the next queued team.
Window gating. Mining happens in time windows where the protocol “releases” a shade. Only one team can occupy a given target at a time (others are queued).

4. Spectral Floor Rule

The Spectral Floor is the core invariant: no on-chain sale may execute below a shade’s highest historical on-chain price.

How the initial floor is set (mined shades)

At mining, the floor equals the fraction of paid licence capacity actually consumed to obtain the colour. Example (start price 10 USD per licence):

  • Team mines R=100, G=30, B=200 → spend fractions 100/255, 30/255, 200/255 of each 10 USD licence.
  • Initial floor ≈ 10×(100/255) + 10×(30/255) + 10×(200/255) = 12.94 USD.

Rounding. Floors are rounded to the nearest cent; accumulated rounding pennies are added to a public “pennies pool” that periodically tops up floors across all colours pro-rata (tiny but fair drift upwards over time).

On-chain enforcement

mapping(uint256 => uint256) public spectralFloor; // colorId => floor price (wei)
require(msg.value >= spectralFloor[colorId], "Below spectral floor");

Transfers that bypass the official market are disabled: token transfers route through the market contract to enforce floor checks.

Neighbourhood protection. High-value neighbours can raise a shade’s effective floor by a small, transparent delta (optional parameter; off in MVP, can be community-governed later).

5. Market Mechanics (Buy/Sell, Direct Purchase)

Sell

Owners can list at or above floor; settlement is non-custodial via the market contract. Floor updates upward at every successful sale.

Buy

Buyers pay listing price + buyer fee; ownership transfers atomically; events are emitted for full transparency.

Direct purchase (Treasury-priced)

The protocol may list select shades at a premium (especially early tiers) to bootstrap treasury and establish reference prices. Direct purchase prices are intentionally high vs mining cost.

6. Splits, Merges & Assemblies

Splits

A shade may be split into 8 children (octave) if its latest sale ≥ T(k), where T(1) = 1,000,000 USDC and T(k)=1,000,000×8^(k-1) for deeper levels. Children inherit the parent floor proportionally.

Merges

Any holder who owns 100% of the direct children can merge them back into the parent; the new floor becomes the weighted average of the children’s floors (by units).

Assemblies

Curated sets (e.g., national flags) can be bound as an Assembly with a fixed assembly fee (e.g., 333,000 USDC → goes to treasury). Dissolution only if the holder owns all parts; fee is not returned.

7. Treasury, Fees & Sustainability

Fees fund audits, security, public goods and operations. No passive yield to users; CoolourCoin is value preservation, not yield.

Fee policy (MVP)

  • Seller fee: 2.5%
  • Buyer fee: 1.0%
  • Burn: 0.2% of trade value (deflationary signal)
  • Audit & security fund: 0.3%

All fees split are on-chain constants in v1.0; changeable only by time-locked governance (or never, if we lock them permanently post-audit).

Treasury uses: contract audits, infrastructure, legal, open-source grants, education. No team promises of dividends or profit sharing.

8. Security & Fairness

Floor enforcement

Only the marketplace contract can transfer shade ownership. Attempted OTC under-floor trades are blocked by design (transfer hooks).

Mining fairness

  • One target at a time: a team can lock only one shade target; other teams queue.
  • Presence proof: live session tokens + device attestation (privacy-preserving). No harvesters, no GPU advantage.
  • Anti-bot windows: release oscillator seeded by live team IDs (+ verifiable randomness). Seed commit-reveal with audit logs.
  • Grace disconnect: 15 minutes; otherwise queue advances.

Open source & audits

Contracts are MIT-licensed, with third-party audits. Critical constants (fee split, floor checks) are non-upgradable or gated by a public time-lock.

9. Compliance Notes

  • No yield, no staking, no “expected profits from the efforts of others”.
  • KYC/AML on fiat on-ramps and large treasury purchases; chain analytics for anomalies.
  • Clear Terms: user licenses, no promise of appreciation, floors ≠ guarantees of liquidity.

Jurisdictions differ. We will consult counsel before mainnet launch.

10. Roadmap (MVP → v1.0)

MVP

  • ERC-721/1155 “Spectral Asset” + Marketplace with floor enforcement.
  • Genesis 4,096 shades (registry JSON), manual windows, basic queue.
  • Licences payment flow (USDC), initial pricing (e.g., 10 USD per channel).
  • Public explorer (read-only): floors, owners, sales.

v0.9

  • Release oscillator (commit-reveal + VRF), anti-bot presence.
  • Splits/Merges; Assemblies with fee.
  • Direct purchase catalogue at premium (treasury-priced).

v1.0

  • Time-lock or lock-in fees; formal audits; Fair-seed proofs.
  • Optional neighbourhood floor; governance minimalism.

Glossary

Spectral Floor: highest on-chain price for a shade; future sales cannot go below it.

Genesis: initial curated set of shades (4,096).

Assembly: named set of colours bound together (e.g., a flag) with its own floor.

Licence: paid right to contribute a channel (R/G/B) to mining during windows.

Appendix: Floor mapping snippet

// Pseudocode (Solidity-like)
mapping(uint256 => uint256) public spectralFloor; // colorId => price in wei

function setInitialFloor(uint256 colorId, uint256 rUsed, uint256 gUsed, uint256 bUsed, uint256 pricePerLicenceWei) internal {
    // rUsed,gUsed,bUsed in [0..255]
    uint256 floorWei =
        (pricePerLicenceWei * rUsed) / 255 +
        (pricePerLicenceWei * gUsed) / 255 +
        (pricePerLicenceWei * bUsed) / 255;
    spectralFloor[colorId] = floorWei;
}

function buy(uint256 colorId) external payable {
    require(msg.value >= spectralFloor[colorId], "Below spectral floor");
    // ... transfer, fee split, emit events, raise floor if msg.value > spectralFloor[colorId]
    if (msg.value > spectralFloor[colorId]) {
        spectralFloor[colorId] = msg.value;
    }
}

In production, transfers route through the market; direct token transfers are blocked or hook-checked to enforce floors.
