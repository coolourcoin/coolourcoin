How CoolourCoin works

1. Genesis Spectrum

CoolourCoin starts from a finite, public set of 4,096 base colours. Think of them as the first layer of a pyramid. Each colour has:

  • a numerical ID;
  • a precise RGB / HEX code;
  • a Tier (1–4), based on its distance to white;
  • a rarity level known from day one.

No new Genesis colours can be invented later. They are published once and remain the backbone of the protocol.

2. Spectral Floor Rule

Every colour has a spectral floor: the highest price it has ever been bought for (in the base currency of the chain, e.g. USDC).

The core rule is enforced in the smart contract: no sale can be executed below that floor.

Technically, the contract keeps a mapping: 

spectralFloor[colorId] = max(spectralFloor[colorId], lastSalePrice);

and requires, on each sale: 

require(salePrice >= spectralFloor[colorId], "Below spectral floor");

This is not marketing language; it is a hard rule. If the contract is live, the floor cannot be bypassed from inside the protocol.

3. Mining by RGB teams

Mining a new colour is not done with GPUs or hash power. It is done by human coordination.

To mine a colour, you need an RGB team:

  • one user with a Red licence (R channel),
  • one user with a Green licence (G channel),
  • one user with a Blue licence (B channel).

All three must be online and connected to the mining server at the same time. They choose together a target colour, e.g. R=120, G=40, B=200.

Each licence has a maximum channel capacity (for example 255 units of R, 255 of G, 255 of B). When the team successfully mines a colour:

  • the used part is consumed from their licences (R-=120, G-=40, B-=200);
  • the colour is minted to the team and assigned an initial floor equal to the fraction of licence value they used.

If any member disconnects and does not return within the safety window, the team is dropped from that mining round and the colour remains unclaimed.

4. Licences and value

Mining licences are the only entry point where fresh value enters the system.

Example:

  • One licence per channel costs 10 USD equivalent.
  • A full RGB team (R + G + B) has 30 USD of mining capacity.

If a team mines a colour using a part of their channels, the initial floor of that colour is proportional to the fraction of licence capacity they spent.

No subsidy, no inflation, no hidden rewards: the protocol only redistributes what users put in, and protects the lowest price each colour has seen.

5. Direct purchase of colours

Not everyone wants to mine. Some users or companies may want to acquire a specific colour directly — for example, the exact shade used in their logo.

The protocol can expose a set of colours for direct purchase at a deterministic price, always:

  • above the expected mining cost,
  • respecting the Spectral Floor Rule,
  • and clearly visible in the UI.

This creates a clean relationship between miners (who discover colours) and buyers (who assign them high value).

6. Treasury and long-term stability

A fraction of licence revenue flows into the CoolourCoin Treasury. The Treasury can:

  • hold colours as a long-term reserve,
  • pay contributors directly in colours,
  • support protocol maintenance and audits.

All Treasury movements are transparent. The protocol does not promise yield. It only keeps an auditable record of where value goes.

7. What CoolourCoin is not

  • It is not a leveraged product.
  • It is not a yield farm.
  • It is not a casino.

CoolourCoin is a way to park value in unique, uncopyable colours, with a floor that cannot be pushed down by panic or manipulation inside the protocol.
