How CoolourCoin works

CoolourCoin is built on three ideas: a finite spectrum of colours, a social way of mining them, and a strict rule that no colour can ever be sold below the highest price it has reached.

1. The Genesis Spectrum

The protocol starts from a finite Genesis Spectrum – a set of 4,096 base colours. Each colour has:

  • a unique ID (colourId),
  • an RGB / HEX code,
  • a tier (from deep shades up to near-white),
  • a spectral floor (initially zero, then updated by the market).

No new base colours are ever minted. Over time, some of them may be split into sub-units, but the total spectrum is fixed: you cannot create additional light out of nowhere.

2. RGB team mining – three people for one shade

To bring a colour to life, you don’t run GPUs – you build a small team. Mining in CoolourCoin requires exactly three independent people:

  • one Red miner (controls the R channel),
  • one Green miner (controls the G channel),
  • one Blue miner (controls the B channel).

Each miner buys a mining licence for their channel (R, G or B). A licence is a fixed budget of “channel intensity” – for example, 255 units of Red that can be spent across multiple colours. Licences are bought with stablecoins (ex. USDC) through the official dApp.

When the protocol announces that a specific colour (R,G,B) is available in a mining window, a team that has at least those values remaining in their licences can lock in:

  • Red commits exactly R units,
  • Green commits exactly G units,
  • Blue commits exactly B units.

If all three commit successfully and stay connected for the required time, the colour is mined and assigned to them. Any unused units in their licences stay available for future colours.

3. How the price floor is created

When a colour is mined by a team, its initial floor is based on how much of each licence was actually used, multiplied by the licence price. In simple terms: the more licence power you spend, the higher the minimum value of that shade.

After mining, the colour can be listed for sale on the official marketplace. Any time a buyer pays a higher price than before, the protocol updates the spectralFloor[colourId].

From that moment on, the rule is hard: no future sale can go below this floor. That’s the core of CoolourCoin.

4. Two ways to obtain a colour

4.1. Mining with friends

You and two others can decide to mine together. Each of you chooses a channel (R, G or B), buys a licence, and waits for mining windows where colours from the current band are released. If you mine successfully, you split the ownership as you all agree (equal shares, or any ratio).

4.2. Direct purchase

If you don’t want to mine, you can buy directly:

  • either colours that other people have mined and listed,
  • or a small set of colours sold directly by the protocol at premium tiers.

Direct sales by the protocol are intentionally priced far above the expected mining cost. This reinforces the idea that mining is the primary way to bring light into the system, while direct purchase is an expensive shortcut for collectors or brands.

5. Splitting and combining colours

Some colours may eventually become very expensive. When a single shade reaches a very high floor (for example 1,000,000 USDC), the protocol can allow it to be split into a fixed number of smaller units (like 8 equal parts).

  • Each part inherits a fraction of the original floor.
  • The original colour is locked, so total value and total light are preserved.
  • If someone later collects all parts again, they can recombine them into a full shade.

This mechanism lets very high-value colours remain usable in smaller pieces, without breaking the trust that the floor represents.

6. What CoolourCoin does not do

To stay honest and simple, the protocol avoids a few common patterns:

  • No staking rewards or passive yield promises.
  • No lotteries, “mystery boxes” or hidden odds.
  • No admin controls that can silently lower floors or confiscate colours.

CoolourCoin is closer to a digital vault for colour than to a speculative game.

Summary:
You can think of CoolourCoin as a way to turn pure colour into a protected digital asset. A finite spectrum, mined socially, with an on-chain rule that says: “never sell below the light it cost.” Everything else – the art, the stories, the brands – sits on top of that rule.
