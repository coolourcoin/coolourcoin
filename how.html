How CoolourCoin works

A colour is a one‑of‑one digital asset. It’s mined by a live RGB team or purchased directly. Either way, its Spectral Floor is enforced forever by the smart contract.

1) RGB Team Mining

Form a team of three — Red, Green, Blue — each with a license. When the protocol releases a target shade (e.g., R200 G128 B099), your team dials exactly those values. If you’re connected and complete, the shade is yours.

  • One shade at a time: a team can mine only a single target concurrently.
  • Exact match: partial or “less than” values don’t mint.
  • Presence rule: if one member disconnects, you have a 15‑minute grace to reconnect, otherwise the slot reopens.
  • Fairness: the system prevents multiple teams from mining the same shade simultaneously (queue with pending state).

2) License Economics

Each channel license has a fixed capacity (0–255). When you mint a shade, you spend that capacity proportionally.

Example: with three 10 USD licenses, minting R100 G30 B200 sets the floor to the sum of used proportions: 10×(100/255) + 10×(30/255) + 10×(200/255) ≈ 12.94 USD. Rounded to the nearest 0.50 by protocol.

3) Direct Purchase

At any time, you can buy colours from the active tier at the protocol price (much higher than the mining input) — ideal for collectors, brands and gifts. The Spectral Floor applies identically.

4) The Spectral Floor (anti‑dump)

For each colour colorId the contract keeps spectralFloor[colorId] in wei. Any transfer with value below that is rejected. Off‑market transfers are blocked by design — trading happens only through the official marketplace.

5) Splits & Merges

  • Split: when a colour exceeds a threshold (e.g., ≥ 1,000,000 USDC last sale), it can split into 8 child units. Each child inherits or increases the parent floor.
  • Merge: if you later own all children, you may merge them back into the parent. The new floor becomes the value‑weighted average.

6) Assemblies (optional)

Own a meaningful set (e.g., your national flag or a brand palette)? Lock it into an Assembly for provenance. A fixed fee (e.g., 333,000) goes to the treasury and the Assembly receives a floor ≥ that fee. Dissolving doesn’t refund the fee; floors persist with the new owners.

7) Fees & Allocation

  • Market fee: 2% (1.25% treasury, 0.5% community pool, 0.25% long‑term reserve).
  • License revenue: to treasury for audits, grants, infrastructure.
  • Rounding gains: accumulated and periodically distributed across colours (pro‑rata to floor).

8) Transparency & Safety

Open‑source contracts, open treasury, public analytics. No yield promises. No admin “god mode”. Anti‑Sybil checks for team mining. Full‑audit before mainnet.
