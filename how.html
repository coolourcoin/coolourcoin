How CoolourCoin Works

Human‑simple. Protocol‑strict. Audit‑clean.

1) Two ways to get a colour

Mine (RGB Team)

Three humans form a team: one Red, one Green, one Blue. Each buys a licence (R/G/B). During an open window the team commits the exact shade values (e.g., R200‑G128‑B099). If all three are online and funded, the colour mints to the team.

Grace rule: if one drops, there is a 15‑minute grace to reconnect; otherwise the window re‑opens to others.

One target at a time: a team can mine only one shade concurrently.

Buy Direct

Purchase a colour straight from the protocol at tier‑based prices. Direct buys are intentionally more expensive than the expected licence spend — miners must have room to make a market.

Direct buys obey the same Spectral Floor rule.

Spectral Floor (Never Below)

Each colour has a permanent price floor equal to its all‑time‑high sale price. Listing below is rejected on‑chain. OTC transfers can be registered via the escrow to update the floor.

2) Licences

Licences are simple spend‑caps per channel. Example: at launch a licence = USD 10 value per channel. When a team mines R100‑G30‑B200, the imputed floor becomes the proportion of spend used:

floor = 10×(100/255) + 10×(30/255) + 10×(200/255) ≈ USD 12.94 → rounded ↑ to USD 13

Unused capacity remains (R155, G225, B55) for the next attempts. We round floors to the nearest cent ↑; rounding slippage is credited to the common pool that lifts all floors weekly.

Issuance: early licences cheaper, later licences rarer + pricier. After a threshold (e.g., last 30% of Genesis) licences are released only via monthly auctions with per‑wallet caps.

3) Splits, Merges & Assemblies

Splits

When a colour crosses a price milestone (e.g., USD 1,000,000), the owner may split it into on‑chain fractions (equal parts). Every part inherits the same floor/metadata.

Merges

If you reunite 100% of the parts, you can merge back into the whole. The resulting floor is the highest paid among all parts.

Assemblies

Bind multiple colours (e.g., a national flag) into one on‑chain asset. A fixed fee (e.g., USD 333,000 to treasury) becomes the assembly’s minimum floor. Dissolve later if you own them all; the fee is not refunded.

4) Market Rules

Anti‑dump

No sale below floor. No admin override. Floors stored on‑chain (e.g., spectralFloor[colorId]).

Treasury

Holds colours & fees. Pays contributors in colours or stablecoins. Can market‑make rare tiers transparently.

Escrow & OTC

OTC trades register via escrow to keep floors accurate; otherwise the protocol won’t update floor records.

Age / KYC guard

Basic age gating (18+). Jurisdictional blocks where required. No passive yield / no airdrops.

Fair randomness

Open‑window oscillator seeded by live team set + blockhash + oracle entropy, revealed after commit (onion RNG). Nobody, including us, can pre‑compute future shades.

Rate limits

Per‑wallet caps for licences; direct‑buy cooldowns on rare tiers; one RGB role per wallet.

5) Why it matters

CoolourCoin is not about hype. It’s about value that won’t dump, represented by something every human understands: colour. The rules are strict, the code is minimal, and the contracts are auditable.
