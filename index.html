What makes CoolourCoin different

Under the visuals, CoolourCoin is a precise, rule-based system: a finite palette, a hard price floor, and a social mining mechanic that requires three independent people to bring a colour into existence.

Rule 1
Spectral Floor – no sell below the light

Every colour has a spectral floor: the highest price ever paid for it. Smart contracts enforce that no future sale can go below this floor. The protocol remembers the peak – and protects it.

Rule 2
Finite Genesis Spectrum

The system starts from a finite set of 4,096 base colours. No new base colours are ever minted. Some colours may later be split into sub-units, but the total light of the spectrum stays constant.

Rule 3
RGB Team Mining

To mine a colour, three people must cooperate: one Red, one Green, one Blue. They connect from different devices and locations and commit exactly the R, G, B values needed. Mining is social by design – not a GPU race.

Rule 4
Two ways to obtain colour

You can either mine with a team using mining licences, or buy directly from the protocol’s current price tiers. Direct purchases are always significantly above the mining cost, reinforcing the floor.

Rule 5
No yield, no hidden promises

CoolourCoin does not promise passive income, APY, staking rewards or airdrops. Its core promise is simpler and more honest: the protocol will never let your colour be sold under its established floor.

Rule 6
Transparent treasury & fees

A fixed part of protocol fees goes into a public treasury that is never used for speculation. The treasury exists to maintain infrastructure, audits and long-term resilience of the protocol.

Rule 7
Origin is permanent

Colours can change hands, but the discovering team is always recorded on-chain. The origin story of each shade – who mined it, when, and with what RGB values – remains visible forever.

Rule 8
Open protocol, auditable logic

The core logic – spectral floors, mining rules, and licence economics – is intended to be open for anyone to inspect, verify and stress-test. If the light is honest, the code must be too.
