How CoolourCoin works

1. Fixed Genesis Spectrum

CoolourCoin starts from a finite, public set of 4,096 base colours. Each colour has:

  • a stable ID,
  • a precise RGB / HEX value,
  • a rarity Tier (1–4), based on distance to white,
  • a known position inside the Genesis pyramid.

No new Genesis colours can be added later. The spectrum is fixed, visible, and can be inspected by anyone.

2. Spectral Floor Rule

Every colour has a spectral floor: the highest price it has ever been bought for on the official marketplace.

The contract logic is simple:

spectralFloor[colorId] = max(spectralFloor[colorId], lastSalePrice);

require(salePrice >= spectralFloor[colorId], "Below spectral floor");

This makes each colour an asset with price memory. Panic selling cannot push it below its own history from inside the protocol.

3. RGB mining teams

New colours are not mined with hash power. They are mined by RGB teams — three people online at the same time:

  • one Red miner (R licence),
  • one Green miner (G licence),
  • one Blue miner (B licence).

Each licence gives a limited channel capacity (for example 255 units of its colour) for a fixed time window. Together, the team chooses a target RGB triple and mines that exact shade.

If any member disconnects and does not return within a short grace period, the team is dropped from that mining attempt. One team can mine only one colour at a time.

4. Licences and initial floor

Licences are the only controlled entry for new value.

Example (simplified):

  • Each licence costs 10 USD equivalent.
  • A full RGB team has 3 licences → 30 USD of mining capacity.

If they mine a colour using a fraction of each licence, the initial floor is proportional to what they actually spent. Rounded amounts (cents etc.) can be directed into protocol reserves to keep accounting clean.

No free inflation, no hidden rewards. You get colours in proportion to what you put in and how you coordinate.

5. Direct colour purchase

Some users prefer to buy a specific colour directly — for example a corporate shade or a national flag combination.

For selected colours, the protocol can offer direct purchase from a pool held by the Treasury. These prices are:

  • always above expected mining cost,
  • always respecting the Spectral Floor Rule,
  • fully visible and deterministic.

6. Treasury and contributors

A defined share of licence revenue goes into a transparent Treasury. The Treasury may:

  • hold rare colours as long-term reserves,
  • pay core contributors directly in colours,
  • support audits, security and protocol maintenance.

Nobody receives yield just for holding. The protocol is designed as a resting place for light, not a yield farm.

7. What CoolourCoin is not

  • It is not a leveraged trading platform.
  • It does not pay interest or “APY”.
  • It does not guarantee profit.

The only guarantee is structural: a colour cannot be sold below its own floor inside the official protocol.
