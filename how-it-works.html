How CoolourCoin works

A finite palette of colours, a strict floor rule, and a social way to mine them. No leverage. No hidden tricks. Just light and rules.

1. Genesis Spectrum — finite from day one

CoolourCoin starts from a Genesis Spectrum of 4 096 base colours. Each base colour:

  • has a fixed ID, HEX / RGB code and a rarity tier,
  • exists only once at that exact coordinate in the spectrum,
  • can later split into sub-blocks, but origin remains traceable.

You are not buying a “token type”. You are buying one specific shade of light.

2. Spectral Floor — the “never below” rule

For every colour, the protocol keeps a single value: its spectral floor.

  • The spectral floor is the highest price ever paid for that colour.
  • Smart contracts block any sale below that floor.
  • No admin key can bypass this rule once deployed.

“Never sell below the light it cost.”

3. RGB Social Mining — three humans, one colour

Mining a colour requires a small RGB team:

  • one Red miner (R licence),
  • one Green miner (G licence),
  • one Blue miner (B licence).

Each miner buys a licence with a maximum channel capacity (for example R=255). When the protocol releases a target colour (e.g. R=100, G=30, B=200), the team must:

  • be online at the same time, from different devices,
  • lock exactly those channel values from their licences,
  • successfully commit the match in the time window.

The percentage of each licence used to catch that colour becomes the minimum value of the colour. The remaining channel capacity stays with the miners for future attempts.

4. Buying colours directly

Not everyone wants to mine. Some people or companies simply want to buy a colour directly:

  • Interface lists available colours from the current mining band.
  • Each colour has a direct purchase price determined by tier & demand.
  • When you buy, your price becomes the new spectral floor.

This allows use cases such as:

  • brands securing “their” colour as a treasury asset,
  • companies gifting a corporate shade to employees,
  • individuals choosing a colour as a long-term store of value.

5. Splitting & merging colour blocks

When a colour reaches certain value thresholds, it can be:

  • split into smaller sub-blocks (e.g. 1 full block → 10×10%),
  • or merged back if someone collects all parts again.

The new block’s floor is computed as the weighted average of all parts. You can pay with fractions of a colour, but the protocol never forgets from which full block they came.

6. No yield, no leverage — just a rest place for value

CoolourCoin does not offer:

  • staking rewards,
  • promised APYs,
  • or leverage mechanisms.

Its only guarantee is: your colour cannot be sold below its own spectral floor. The rest is market, community and time.

7. Why it matters

Most crypto assets are built around speculation and variance. CoolourCoin is built around preservation and visibility:

  • what you own is visible (a colour you can see),
  • how far it can fall is bounded (the floor),
  • how it is mined is social, not industrial.

It is not a casino. It is an instrument of rest in an otherwise volatile space.
